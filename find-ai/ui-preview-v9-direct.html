Why drop the Ready screen

The Ready screen was introduced to preview the deliverable ("Trust Grade A–D") and list what we'd ask. It sounded reassuring in isolation, but in flow it behaves like a "are you sure you want to do the thing you just said you want to do?" prompt. That's friction, not trust.

What replaces it, without losing reassurance:

  • The tile itself already previews the job (emoji + name + user's own question).
  • The pick screen subtitle sets the time expectation: "Pick one. Goes straight in — no extra step."
  • The tool's first screen carries its own kicker (gold dot + "Check a tenant") + progress bar (1/4) + a footnote ("4 questions · ~90 seconds") — all the Ready-screen reassurance, embedded where the real work starts.
  • The Trust Grade promise shows up naturally at the end — as the artifact, not as a waiting-room preview.
