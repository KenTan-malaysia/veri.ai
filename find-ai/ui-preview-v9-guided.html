v9 Guided Flow · Principles
01 · One thing per screen
Screen 1 asks nothing. Screen 2 asks ONE question. Screen 3 previews ONE task.

A 5-year-old can follow this. No competing elements. No menus. No stats. Just: "what do you want?" → "here's what will happen" → "start."

02 · Progress bar (3 dots)
User always knows where they are.

Three dots at the top of every screen. "You are on 2 of 3." Reduces anxiety — people stay to the end when they know it ends.

03 · Plain human language
"Check a tenant" — not "Tenant Screening Report."

Every label is how a friend would describe it. No jargon. No product names on the front surface. "SDSAS" becomes "How much do I owe LHDN?"

04 · Big thumb targets
Giant emoji + giant button + giant spacing.

Every tap target ≥56px tall. Emojis carry meaning faster than words. Back button is a ring, not text. Everything thumb-reachable.

The key shift
From "show everything" to "ask one thing."

v8 showed all four tools at once. v9 asks "what do you want?" first, then shows ONLY that tool. Same 4 tools underneath — but the user never sees 3 of them on the first screen. Zero cognitive load. Typeform / Duolingo / Headspace all structure onboarding this way.
